# v1.4.1 — The router learns to read the price (stable)

**Tag:** `v1.4.1` · **Date:** 2026-06-27 · **Previous:** `v1.4.0` (2026-06-25), rc line: `1.4.1-rc.1` (learned-cost re-rank)

The **stable v1.4.1**. Promotes `1.4.1-rc.1` to final. One slice: the effective-cost track's
**confidence-gated learned-cost re-rank** (decision **d060**) — the d026 "router that learns" payoff.
v1.4.0 made the fleet *measure* what it actually paid for the quality it got; v1.4.1 lets routing
*act* on that signal. Both test gates are green and there are **0 open issues**. The rc line ends here.

---

## Theme

v1.4.0 closed the measurement loop: Baton folds Acceptance-Gate verdicts and spend into a per-worker
**effective-cost leaderboard** (`effective_cost = realized_cost ÷ realized_quality`; lower is better),
surfaced advisory-only via `/baton:effective-cost`. The leaderboard *knew* which workers were cheap
on paper but expensive once rejects and polish rounds were counted — but routing couldn't see it.

**v1.4.1 wires that learned signal into routing.** When you opt in, `Select-Capability` biases its
economy ranking by each worker's learned effective cost: a worker that has cost more per unit quality
**yields toward the next cost tier**; a learned-cheap worker reaches toward the tier below. This is the
*Price Reversal Phenomenon* thesis (arXiv 2603.23971) made operational — listed per-token price is an
unreliable proxy for realized cost, and now the router corrects for it. North star unchanged:
**autonomy + legibility**, with **cost-optimization** as the through-line.

## What's new since v1.4.0

### Confidence-gated learned-cost re-rank (PR #69; decision d060; `1.4.1-rc.1`)

When the box-private top-level switch `learned_routing: true` is set, `Select-Capability` biases its
**economy** ranking by each worker's learned `eff_cost_mean` (folded across the per-run
`effective-cost.json` records). The mechanism mirrors the saturation driver's advisory-first discipline:

- **Bounded adjacent-tier (d060).** The learned signal shifts a worker's effective tier rank by at most
  ±`MaxShift` (default 1.0): a learned-expensive worker yields toward the next tier up, a learned-cheap
  one reaches toward the tier below. It can **never** move a worker two tiers, and **never** below the
  saturation driver's −1 floor. This captures the headline "cheap-but-rejected is actually expensive"
  case while keeping the cost tier as a budget guardrail.
- **Confidence-gated.** Only leaderboard rows at/above `MinConfidence` (0.5 — the bar the leaderboard
  already flags `tentative` below) move routing or anchor the **peer median** (which excludes the
  evaluated worker, so a worker is compared against its alternatives, not itself).
- **Default-OFF, byte-for-byte.** Switch unset/false → every candidate's `learned_adjust = 0.0` and
  `Get-LearnedTierRank … 0 ≡ Get-EffectiveTierRank` → ranking is **identical to v1.4.0**. Verified by a
  test that ranks the same fleet with the switch off *while records are present*.
- **Economy-only.** Champion mode ("just the best quality", cost-blind) never reads the board.
- **Box-private.** The leaderboard folds from the injectable `-RunsRoot` (`$BATON_HOME/runs`) and never
  leaves the box; the shared `references/fleet.yaml` carries only a commented field doc for
  `learned_routing`.

New pure functions: `Get-LearnedCostAdjustment` (bounded, confidence-weighted bias),
`Get-LearnedTierRank` (saturation-floored effective rank), `Read-EffectiveCostRecords` (shared
`-RunsRoot`/`-Glob` reader), `Get-LearnedRoutingEnabled` (strict-opt-in switch). `Select-Capability`
gains an injectable `-RunsRoot` seam and the §3c learned-cost step.

The realized-cost `CostResolver` (token/dollar metering to replace the estimate basis) remains the named,
deferred next step — it would flip the bias from estimate-anchored to measured.

## Test status

All gates green: `effective-cost-lib` (incl. `Get-LearnedCostAdjustment`, `Read-EffectiveCostRecords`,
`Get-LearnedRoutingEnabled`), `fleet-effective-cost` (incl. C14/C15 `--runs` glob), `saturation-lib`
(incl. `Get-LearnedTierRank` L1–L4), `routing-lib` (off-by-default byte-for-byte W_off1, the learned
flip W_on1, champion invariance W_champ1), `routing-dispatch`, `conductor-lib`, bootstrap. Deployed live
and smoke-verified: with `learned_routing` off → `localw` ranks first (tier ordinal, unchanged); on → a
learned-terrible `localw` yields to a learned-great `freew` (`why`: *learned eff_cost 1.00 vs fleet
median 50.00 (conf 1.00) → −1 tier*).

### Review

Final adversarial review returned **CHANGES REQUIRED**: **1 Critical** — lifting the record reader into
the shared lib silently dropped the CLI's `--runs` glob (fixed with a `-Glob` mode; caught by the
adversarial review, not the unit tests); **1 Important** — `0/0 = NaN` at `MinConfidence = 1.0` slipped
past the weight clamp (denominator guard added). Two Minors fixed; **one Minor rejected** with empirical
proof — re-adding `@()` around the leaderboard assignment re-nests the unary-comma return and silently
kills the per-row lookup. A focused confirmation review returned **CONFIRMED**.
