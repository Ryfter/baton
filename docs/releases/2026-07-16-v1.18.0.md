# v1.18.0 — Usage-aware failover: the fleet routes around its own limits (d083 + d090, #94)

**Released:** 2026-07-16 · **PRs:** #103 (slice 1, `fcfc1df`) + #107 (Layer 2, `f4ee8ee`) ·
**Issues:** #94 closed · **Decisions:** d083 (reactive-first), d090 (buffer policy) ·
Also on this release: #105 conductor assets (`4aad37d`), ship-report spec (`3f3b7e6`).

Born from three Codex cap-deaths in two days (2026-07-15/16), two of them mid-build on this
very feature. The fleet now classifies its own failures, routes around exhausted providers,
and — with Layer 2 — checks headroom *before* launching a doomed job.

## Slice 1 — reactive failover (the correctness layer)

- **`scripts/usage-classify-lib.ps1`** — classifies every failed dispatch's exit +
  stdout/stderr into `quota_exhausted | rate_limit_burst | auth_config | server_overload |
  ambiguous`, with parsed `reset_at`. Auth is evaluated **first** and never enters the
  failover path; ambiguous fails open to a short cooldown, never exhaustion; no parseable
  reset → bounded TTL (6h/1h), never permanent lockout.
- **Usage Governor journal events** (`source='error_classify'`) — lockout/cooldown rows in
  the existing journal; no parallel store.
- **Route-around** — lockouts make `Select-Capability` skip the dead worker.
- **ONE substitute retry** at the executor: primary locked *before* the retry, substitute
  re-resolves the v1.17.0 stakes/depth policy (a failover hop cannot bypass stakes caps),
  `quality_first` posture (equal-or-better peer or a loud "no peer available"), attempted-
  provider set, one plain-language operator line per hop.

## Layer 2 — pre-flight buffer policy (the prevention layer)

- **Codex app-server probe** (`scripts/usage-probe-lib.ps1`) — headless JSON-RPC
  `account/rateLimits/read` → used% + reset for the 5-hour and weekly windows. ~10min TTL
  cache; **fail-open on any failure** — a probe can never block dispatch. (Fork U-A resolved
  YES by live experiment 2026-07-16.)
- **`usage_policy` knobs** per provider (box-private; seed ships placeholders):
  `soft_cap_5h` (default 75), `soft_cap_weekly` (default 85), `monthly_allowance`
  (user-entered — GitHub avatar → Copilot Settings → usage), `probe`.
- **Pre-flight at the dispatch seam:** over a soft cap → no auto-dispatch; loud operator
  line naming every crossed window; falls through to slice 1's substitute path with the
  substitute **re-probed** (both over cap = loud hold — no silent cap bypass). Journaled as
  `preflight` events.
- **"Will this job fit" advisory** (observe-only): median tokens/dispatch from journal
  history vs remaining headroom.
- **Surplus-spend preference:** weekly reset <24h + >20 points headroom → a 1e-7 near-tie
  breaker that provably cannot flip a real quality difference (tested at 0.90 vs 0.85).

## How it was built (the fleet story)

Slice 1: Codex built (cap-died during its final report — work intact), Grok reviewed
DO-NOT-SHIP on the classifier regex layer (auth-after-quota ordering; over-broad patterns)
and implemented its own fixes. Layer 2: dispatched **through its own pre-flight** (probe:
0% / 56%, under caps — first dogfood), Codex built clean, Grok reviewed SHIP-WITH-TWEAKS
(5/5 findings real) and fixed its own findings. A Gemini audition ran the identical
adversarial prompt: 1/4 real with 2 hallucinated Criticals — Grok remains the adversarial
tier, now measured. 41 local-model lens findings adjudicated across both PRs: 2 real.
